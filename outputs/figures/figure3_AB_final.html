Figure 3B. Daily Discourse Volume by Group (포스터 / 발표용)

Figure 3B. Daily total post volume across three discourse groups — FM_Direct (blue), Court (orange), and Chronic (purple) — presented as stacked bars from February 7 to March 21, 2026. Vertical dashed lines mark E1 (red; BAI audit disclosure, February 23) and E2 (amber; SBS broadcast on court-recognized causal link between COVID-19 vaccination and myocardial infarction death, March 2). Shaded regions denote Pre-E1, E1→E2, and Post-E2 periods.

Key patterns. Prior to E1 (Pre-E1 period), daily post volume was low and relatively stable across all groups (approximately 10–30 posts/day total), confirming the absence of major discourse activation before the BAI audit disclosure. Following E1, FM_Direct volume surged dramatically — peaking above 430 posts on February 23 itself — driven entirely by the audit disclosure. This represents the primary discourse activation event of the study period. In the E1→E2 window, FM_Direct volume remained elevated while Court volume began to increase in anticipation of the SBS broadcast. Following E2, a secondary surge occurred on March 2 (peak ~385 posts), this time driven jointly by FM_Direct and Court posts — reflecting the judicial ruling's amplification of both foreign matter and litigation discourse simultaneously. Post-E2, FM_Direct maintained a sustained elevated volume (~200–280 posts/day), while Court volume tapered, and Chronic provided a steady background contribution throughout. Notably, political events (March 10 parliamentary hearing; March 13 political figure meeting) did not produce comparable volume surges, confirming that discourse activation was selective for institutional disclosures from independent bodies (audit agencies, courts) rather than political amplification.

Total posts: 4,780 across 43 days (Feb 7 – Mar 21, 2026). FM_Direct: 2,418; Court: 585; Chronic: 1,867. After relevance filtering.

Figure 3A. Daily Discourse Volume and C1/C7 Prevalence by Group (논문용)

Figure 3A. Daily total post volume (bars, left axis) and prevalence of C1 Confidence (solid line) and C7 Conspiracy (dashed line, right axis) for each discourse group: FM_Direct (blue), Court (orange), and Chronic (purple). Vertical dashed lines mark E1 (red; BAI audit disclosure, February 23) and E2 (amber; SBS broadcast, March 2). Lines connect days with data; gaps indicate no posts collected on that date.

Key patterns. (1) FM_Direct: Post volume surged immediately at E1 and remained elevated. C1 prevalence rose above 80% within days of E1 and sustained, while C7 declined from a high pre-event baseline (~60–70%) to the 20–30% range — reflecting compositional dilution of pre-existing conspiracy content by event-specific institutional distrust discourse. The concurrent volume explosion and C1 surge confirm rapid, event-driven activation of targeted institutional distrust. (2) Court: Volume concentrated in the pre-E1 period (pre-existing litigation discourse) with a secondary surge at E2. C1 spiked above 80% after E2 and remained persistently elevated through March 21. Critically, C7 collapsed to near-zero in the E1→E2 window and showed only partial recovery post-E2 (~10–20%), confirming discourse specificization: with both volume and C1 high but C7 essentially absent, the post-E2 Court corpus consists almost entirely of targeted institutional accountability discourse rather than conspiracy content. (3) Chronic: Volume co-elevated with both events, consistent with the amplifier role. C1 rose progressively from ~40% to ~70% across the full period. C7 showed a broad declining trend from Pre-E1 highs (~60–80%), reflecting dilution by C1-dominant content — but the absolute C7 volume (bar × C7 line) remained non-trivial throughout, consistent with a persistent conspiracy background in long-term skepticism communities.

Left axis: daily total posts per group. Right axis: C1/C7 prevalence (%). Days with zero posts are omitted from prevalence lines. C2 Complacency omitted (prevalence <1%). C3 Constraints, C5 Collective responsibility not in CAVES→7C mapping.
