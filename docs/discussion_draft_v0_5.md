A 7C Framework Analysis of Korean Social Media Discourse*

**4. Discussion**

This study examined how 7C-type distributions of vaccine hesitancy
discourse changed before and after the February 23, 2026 Board of Audit
and Inspection (BAI) audit disclosure across two Naver social media
channels (blog posts and news article comments). Using a validated
LLM-based classification pipeline applied to 5,973 Korean-language
posts, we identified event-specific shifts in the co-occurrence
architecture of vaccine hesitancy dimensions, with particular salience
for the Confidence (C1)--Conspiracy (C7) cluster. In this section, we
interpret the empirical findings in light of Korea\'s unique
institutional context and discuss the theoretical and practical
implications for public health communication.

**4.1 Principal Findings**

***4.1.1 Event-Specific 7C Activation Profiles***

The empirical findings partially supported our primary hypothesis while
revealing an unexpected and theoretically significant pattern in the
Court discourse group. In the FM_Direct group --- dominated by keywords
directly linked to the BAI audit (foreign matter in vaccines, fungal
contamination) --- C1 prevalence increased dramatically from 21.6%
pre-event to 75.0% post-event (+53.4 percentage points, OR=10.90,
p\<0.001), confirming the predicted activation of institutional
Confidence concerns. C7 showed a modest and non-significant increase
(21.6% to 25.0%, OR=1.21, p=0.74), suggesting that the audit disclosure
activated targeted institutional distrust (C1) far more strongly than
generalized conspiracy beliefs (C7).\
The Court group exhibited a strikingly different and
counter-hypothesized pattern: C7 decreased substantially from 39.6%
pre-event to 13.8% post-event (-25.8 percentage points, OR=0.24,
p\<0.001), while C1 surged from 6.0% to 45.9% (+39.9pp, OR=13.31,
p\<0.001) and C4 increased from 0.7% to 4.1% (OR=6.05, p\<0.001).
Contrary to the hypothesis of C1+C6+C7 co-activation, the SBS news broadcast (March 2) --- reporting the court's recognition of a causal link between COVID-19 vaccination and myocardial infarction death, with KDCA's subsequent appeal as secondary context --- appears to have displaced rather than amplified conspiracy discourse. We interpret this pattern as a discourse specificization effect: the judicial event, as mediated through mass-media framing, converted diffuse conspiratorial framing into targeted institutional distrust directed at a specific agency and a specific causal claim. The structural basis for the
unexpectedly high pre-event C7 in the Court group is examined in Section
4.1.4. The Chronic group demonstrated sustained C1 co-elevation across
both trigger events (+32.0pp, OR=4.88, p\<0.001), confirming its role as
a background amplifier, with modest but significant increases also
observed in C4 (+2.7pp, p=0.007) and C6 (+3.3pp, p\<0.001).

***4.1.2 The C1--C7 Co-occurrence Pattern***

A central empirical finding of this study concerns the C1+C7 co-labeling
rate and its differential trajectory across groups. In the FM_Direct
group, C1+C7 co-occurrence increased from 17.6% pre-event to 24.4%
post-event (1.4-fold), though this increase did not reach statistical
significance (p=0.32), likely due to the small pre-event stratum (n=51).
Structurally, the FM_Direct post-event C1+C7 architecture is notable: of
1,423 post-event posts, 50.6% carried C1 alone, 24.4% carried C1+C7
jointly, and only 0.6% carried C7 alone --- indicating that C7 in this
context is almost entirely embedded within C1, consistent with the
proposed evidence-backed conspiracy catalysis mechanism (Section 4.3).
In the Court and Chronic groups, C1+C7 co-occurrence increased
significantly post-event (Court: 2.8% to 6.2%, 2.2-fold, p\<0.001;
Chronic: 7.3% to 12.5%, 1.7-fold, p\<0.001), despite --- or perhaps
because of --- the overall C7 decrease in the Court group, suggesting
that residual C7 became more tightly coupled with C1 as the post-event
discourse concretized around specific institutional failures.

***4.1.3 Discourse Independence and the Amplifier Role of Chronic
Distrust***

The two trigger events activated largely independent discourse pathways,
with only approximately 5% overlap between FM_Direct and Court posts
(confirmed by exploratory analysis prior to classification). This
independence is reflected in their divergent 7C profiles: FM_Direct
predominantly activated C1 (+53.4pp), while Court activated a C1 surge
accompanied by C7 displacement and C4 increase. The Chronic group ---
spanning chronic baseline distrust keywords --- co-elevated with both
events, showing significant C1 increases (+32.0pp, p\<0.001) and modest
but significant increases in C4 and C6. This pattern confirms the
hypothesized amplifier role: pre-existing distrust does not simply
persist as a static background but dynamically co-activates with
event-specific hesitancy responses, modulating their intensity across
the discourse ecosystem. This amplifier mechanism aligns with Betsch et
al.\'s (2018) conceptualization of Confidence as a cumulative
attitudinal construct.

***4.1.4 Global--Local C7 Displacement: Transnational Conspiracy
Networks in Korean Vaccine Discourse***

Corpus-level analysis revealed that the high pre-event C7 prevalence in
the Court group (39.6%) was substantially attributable to global
anti-vaccine conspiracy content circulating on Naver Blog prior to the
trigger events. Keyword-channel breakdown showed that the \'코로나 백신
소송\' (COVID-19 vaccine lawsuit) keyword in the blog channel carried a
C7 prevalence of 54.7% pre-event, compared with 7.7% in the news comment
channel for the same keyword. Qualitative inspection of rationale
outputs confirmed that this blog content predominantly referenced global
conspiratorial narratives --- World Economic Forum, Chinese Communist
Party involvement, David Icke, nanomaterial injection theories ---
rather than Korean-specific institutional critique. An estimated 53.8%
of Court pre-event C7-positive posts reflected global rather than
Korean-specific conspiracy framing. This finding has two implications.
First, it demonstrates that Korean vaccine hesitancy discourse is not an
isolated local phenomenon but is embedded within transnational
anti-vaccine information networks --- a finding with direct relevance
for infodemic surveillance design. Second, it means that the apparent
post-event C7 decrease in the Court group reflects not a genuine
reduction in conspiratorial belief but a compositional shift: the
post-event influx of Korea-specific C1-dominant content (judicial
proceedings, SBS broadcast of the court ruling) numerically diluted the pre-existing global C7
background, while absolute C7 volume remained substantial.

The discourse specificization effect observed in the Court group ---
where post-event C7 declined sharply as event-specific C1 surged ---
provides an important qualification to evidence-backed conspiracy
catalysis theory. Not all official disclosures of institutional failure
produce C7 amplification. The SBS broadcast (March 2) did not introduce
a new allegation --- it translated an existing judicial finding into
mass-media framing, converting a legal outcome into a publicly legible
causal claim: COVID-19 vaccines cause myocardial infarction. When a
media event provides a concrete, named institutional actor (KDCA) and a
specific causal claim rendered in lay terms, public discourse appears to
redirect from diffuse conspiratorial framing toward targeted
institutional accountability demands. This suggests a distinction
between two types of official disclosure events: audit-type disclosures,
which reveal systemic opacity and thereby feed C7 by validating \'they
knew and concealed it\' narratives; and judicial-type disclosures, which
provide specific causal adjudication that channels distrust into C1 and
C4 rather than C7. The temporal co-occurrence of both event types in the
present study --- with only nine days separating the BAI audit (February
23) and the SBS broadcast (March 2) --- provides a unique natural
experiment for observing these divergent mechanisms within the same
population and time window.

**4.2 The Institutional Capture of the 7C Communication Channel: A
Structural Interpretation**

The high and increasing C1+C7 co-occurrence observed in Korean vaccine
discourse cannot be adequately explained by individual psychological
processes alone. We propose that the observed pattern reflects a
structural condition we term institutional capture of the 7C
communication channel --- a condition in which the institutional
architecture responsible for vaccine safety governance systematically
undermines the credibility of safety-related communications, thereby
converting ordinary distrust (C1) into conspiracy-adjacent belief (C7).

***4.2.1 The Surveillance--Compensation Structural Trap***

In most high-income countries, vaccine pharmacovigilance and injury
compensation are institutionally separated. Active surveillance systems
(e.g., the US v-safe program, the UK Yellow Card scheme) are operated
independently of compensation adjudication bodies, preserving the
epistemic independence of safety signal generation. In South Korea,
however, the Korea Disease Control and Prevention Agency (KDCA)
simultaneously administers passive safety surveillance, compensation
review, and public communication on vaccine safety --- functions whose
co-location creates an inherent credibility deficit.

The consequence of this co-location is what we term contextual
non-acceptance: any KDCA scientific statement on vaccine safety is
structurally interpreted through the lens of its
compensation-adjudicating role, such that evidence of safety is received
as institutional self-interest rather than objective assessment. This
dynamic is not specific to bad-faith actors --- it is a rational
Bayesian inference on the part of the public when the same institution
generates both safety evidence and compensation decisions.

***4.2.2 The Evidence Vacuum and Judicial Substitution***

The absence of a robust active surveillance infrastructure compounded
this structural trap. Without prospective cohort data, signal detection
studies, or population-level pharmacoepidemiological analyses, the KDCA
lacked the evidentiary capacity to respond to causality claims with
scientific authority. Into this evidence vacuum, the judicial system was
called upon to adjudicate vaccine causality --- a role for which courts
are institutionally ill-equipped, as legal standards of causality
(presumption, balance of probabilities) diverge fundamentally from
epidemiological standards (attributable risk, confidence intervals,
dose-response relationships).

The March 2026 SBS broadcast reporting the court's recognition of a causal link between COVID-19 vaccination and myocardial infarction death --- and the subsequent KDCA appeal --- crystallized this dynamic. The court\'s presumption-based
ruling, issued in the absence of KDCA-generated active surveillance
data, was publicly received as scientific validation of vaccine harm.
The KDCA\'s appeal, in turn, was interpreted not as a legitimate
scientific objection but as institutional self-protection, further
eroding C1 and catalyzing C7.

A further consequence of the evidence vacuum is the qualitative
transformation of the Calculation dimension (C4). In the original 7C
framework, C4 denotes exploratory cost-benefit analysis in which
individuals weigh the personal risks and benefits of vaccination using
available scientific data (Betsch et al., 2018). In the Korean context
of 2026, this function has undergone a structural shift: as
KDCA-generated safety data became contextually non-credible ---
perceived as compensation-motivated rather than scientifically motivated
--- the public\'s C4 calculation progressively excluded official agency
data as a valid input variable. In its place, audit disclosures,
judicial rulings, and community-sourced injury accounts assumed the role
of primary evidence. We term this shift the C1→C4 cascade: when
institutional Confidence collapses (C1), Calculation does not cease but
reconfigures around non-institutional information sources, producing
what we characterize as defensive or judgmental calculation --- oriented
not toward vaccination decision-making but toward causal attribution,
systemic verification, and identification of legal remedies. This
reconfigured C4 is empirically distinguishable from its exploratory
antecedent: it is predicted to co-occur with C1 and C7 at substantially
higher rates, and to reference judicial or audit authority rather than
epidemiological evidence.

***4.2.3 The Self-Reinforcing Distrust Loop***

These dynamics constitute a closed, self-reinforcing loop that operates
independently of individual vaccination decisions:

> \(1\) KDCA performs surveillance + compensation simultaneously
>
> \(2\) Active surveillance capacity atrophies → evidence vacuum
>
> \(3\) Courts fill the vacuum via presumption-based causality rulings
>
> \(4\) Judicial rulings are received as scientific authority (C7
> legitimation)
>
> \(5\) KDCA scientific rebuttals are discounted as self-interested (C1
> deepening)
>
> \(6\) Public demand for active surveillance grows, but institutional
> identity remains compensation-centered → return to (1)

This loop predicts that in societies where surveillance and compensation
are institutionally co-located, C1 and C7 will be structurally
co-produced --- not as a psychological accident but as a necessary
consequence of the institutional architecture. The present data, showing

a **\[X.X-fold\]** post-event increase in C1+C7 co-occurrence following
an official government audit confirming safety management failures,
provide the first empirical evidence consistent with this structural
prediction in a real-world setting.

**4.3 The BAI Audit as Evidence-Backed Conspiracy Catalyst**

The 7C framework\'s Conspiracy dimension (C7) was originally
conceptualized as a belief in malicious intent unsupported by evidence
--- a departure from rational risk assessment into the domain of
unfalsifiable narrative (Geiger et al., 2021). The present findings
complicate this picture in two directions. In the FM_Direct group, the
BAI audit disclosure provided official documentary support for the
narrative that \'the government knew and concealed it\': 1,285 foreign
matter reports were confirmed to have been withheld from the Ministry of
Food and Drug Safety, and 1.31 million doses were administered without
standard lot release testing. In this context, C7 narratives acquired
evidentiary scaffolding from the government\'s own audit --- a process
we term evidence-backed conspiracy catalysis. Importantly, however, the
FM_Direct data show that the primary post-event response was C1
activation (21.6% to 75.0%) rather than C7 amplification (21.6% to
25.0%, ns), suggesting that official confirmation of institutional
failure drives targeted institutional distrust more powerfully than
generalized conspiracy belief. C7 in the FM_Direct post-event corpus was
almost entirely co-embedded with C1 (C1+C7: 24.4%; C7 alone: 0.6%),
consistent with conspiracy belief functioning as an extension of ---
rather than alternative to --- institutional distrust.

We term this dynamic evidence-backed conspiracy catalysis: a process by
which official disclosures of institutional failure provide evidentiary
scaffolding for pre-existing conspiracy narratives, lowering the
psychological cost of transitioning from C1 (distrust) to C7
(conspiratorial belief). This distinction matters for public health
communication: conventional conspiracy inoculation strategies
(Lewandowsky & van der Linden, 2021) target psychologically motivated
reasoning against contrary evidence. Evidence-backed conspiracy
catalysis represents a different challenge --- one in which the evidence
itself is the vector, and inoculation against the narrative risks
appearing to deny the underlying institutional failures.\
The Court group, by contrast, illustrates a second mechanism: discourse
specificization. When a judicial event provides a named institutional
actor and a specific causal claim, diffuse conspiratorial framing is
displaced by targeted institutional distrust (C1) and defensive
calculation (C4). The pre-event Court C7 was disproportionately composed
of global anti-vaccine conspiracy content (transnational networks
referencing WEF, CCP, nanomaterials), which was numerically overwhelmed
by Korea-specific C1 content following the SBS broadcast. Together,
these two mechanisms --- catalysis in FM_Direct, specificization in
Court --- suggest that the relationship between institutional disclosure
events and C7 is not uniform but depends critically on the type of
disclosure: audit-type events feed C7 by validating opacity narratives,
while judicial-type events channel distrust into C1 and C4 by providing
concrete, attributable institutional actors.

The appropriate institutional response in this context is not
communication strategy revision but structural accountability: only
genuine evidence generation through independent active surveillance can
interrupt the C1→C7 transition pathway. As long as the KDCA cannot
produce prospective pharmacoepidemiological data that stands
independently of its compensation function, its communications will
remain structurally non-credible regardless of content.

**4.4 Implications for the 7C Framework**

The 7C framework was developed primarily as a model of individual
psychological antecedents of vaccination behavior (Geiger et al., 2021;
Betsch et al., 2018). The present findings suggest that in contexts of
sustained institutional failure, the 7C dimensions are not solely
psychological states but can be jointly produced by institutional
architecture. Specifically, our findings suggest four extensions to the
existing framework.

First, institutional antecedents of C1 and C7 co-occurrence: the
surveillance--compensation co-location creates a structural precondition
for C1+C7 co-production that is independent of individual risk
perception. Future applications of the 7C framework in institutional
trust contexts should consider measuring institutional structure
variables alongside individual psychological constructs.

Second, the judicial authority transfer variable: in the absence of
credible institutional evidence generation, publics may transfer
scientific authority to judicial bodies. This transfer --- measurable as
increased reliance on court rulings over agency communications as
reference points for vaccine safety --- represents a novel antecedent of
C7 not captured by the original framework. Possible operationalization:
\'I trust the court\'s judgment on vaccine safety more than KDCA\'s
official position\' as a survey item predicting C7 scores.

Third, evidence-backed conspiracy as a distinct C7 subtype: standard C7
measurement instruments (e.g., 5C/7C scales) do not distinguish between
conspiracy beliefs grounded in official disclosures of institutional
failure and those lacking any evidentiary basis. This distinction has
direct implications for intervention design: the former requires
institutional repair, while the latter is amenable to psychological
inoculation approaches.

Fourth, the temporal transformation of C4 --- from exploratory to
defensive calculation: standard 7C instruments measure C4 as individual
cost-benefit reasoning at the point of vaccination decision. The present
findings suggest that in post-crisis contexts, C4 may undergo a
qualitative transformation in which the information inputs,
psychological motivation, and behavioral outputs of the calculative
process are fundamentally altered. Specifically, when institutional
Confidence (C1) collapses and official data loses contextual
credibility, C4 reconfigures from exploratory uncertainty management
toward defensive system verification --- using judicial, audit, and
community sources rather than epidemiological evidence as its primary
inputs. This transformed C4 is characterized by co-occurrence with C1
and C7, and by orientation toward causal attribution and legal remedy
rather than prospective vaccination decisions. Future 7C measurement
instruments applied in post-crisis institutional trust contexts should
consider distinguishing between these two qualitatively distinct C4
subtypes.

Fifth, the behavior--attitude decoupling problem and the limits of C6:
the 7C framework implicitly assumes that observed vaccination behavior
reflects underlying attitudinal acceptance. However, Korea\'s high
vaccination coverage was achieved under conditions of mandatory vaccine
pass (방역패스) policies and pervasive social media-based peer
visibility of vaccination status---a participatory surveillance
environment in which individuals simultaneously function as data
contributors and horizontal monitors of each other\'s health behaviors
(Albrechtslund, 2008). Under such conditions, elevated C6 (Compliance)
scores in discourse data may reflect not voluntary social norm alignment
but structural coercion and horizontal peer pressure, producing
behavioral compliance that is decoupled from attitudinal acceptance.
This decoupling limits the inferential value of C6 as a predictor of
genuine vaccination readiness in mandate-heavy public health contexts,
and suggests that future 7C applications should distinguish between
compliance grounded in attitudinal acceptance and compliance produced by
institutional or social coercion.

Sixth, the retroactive reframing of coerced compliance as a structural
source of grievance discourse. The 7C framework treats vaccination as a
prospective decision point, but does not account for what may be termed
*post-hoc grievance reframing*: the retrospective reconstruction of a
completed vaccination act as involuntary when adverse outcomes
materialize. In the Korean context, this dynamic was structurally
enabled by dual external pressures that compromised the substantive
voluntariness of consent despite formal procedural compliance: the
institutional coercion of the vaccine pass system (방역패스),
which conditioned access to workplaces and public facilities on
vaccination status (Bardosh et al., 2022), and the horizontal
conformity pressure of social media-based peer surveillance (Albrechtslund,
2008). When adverse events subsequently occurred, the gap between
procedural consent and experienced coercion became the basis for a
grievance narrative combining C1 (loss of confidence in safety
assurances), C4 (retrospective recalculation of an externally imposed
decision), and C6 (compliance experienced as coercion) --- a pattern
that standard 7C instruments, calibrated to prospective vaccination
intention, are structurally unable to capture. A fuller theoretical
analysis of this phenomenon, including its historical and comparative
dimensions, is developed elsewhere (Cheon, in preparation).

**4.5 Practical Implications for Public Health Communication**

The structural analysis presented above has direct implications for KDCA
communication strategy in the aftermath of the BAI audit disclosure.
Three practical recommendations follow from the empirical and
theoretical findings.

First, message credibility requires structural precondition.
Communication interventions targeting C1 (e.g., corrective messages
about vaccine safety, transparent disclosure of adverse event data) will
have limited efficacy as long as the KDCA simultaneously adjudicates
compensation. Structural separation of active surveillance from
compensation functions --- as practiced in the United States
(VAERS/v-safe vs. CICP) and the United Kingdom (Yellow Card vs. Vaccine
Damage Payment Scheme) --- is a prerequisite for restoring contextual
acceptance of safety communications.

Second, acknowledge the evidentiary legitimacy of distrust. In the
context of evidence-backed conspiracy catalysis, communication
strategies that deny or minimize the BAI audit findings will accelerate
C7 formation. Proactive acknowledgment of institutional failures, paired
with concrete structural remediation commitments, is more likely to
interrupt the C1→C7 transition than defensive messaging.

Third, group-specific communication targeting. The distinct 7C profiles
of FM_Direct (C1+C7) and Court (C1+C6+C7) groups indicate that
one-size-fits-all communication is insufficient. FM_Direct audiences
require transparency on safety management procedures and independent
surveillance capacity; Court audiences additionally require
acknowledgment of the tension between judicial presumption standards and
epidemiological causality standards, and a credible commitment to
providing the scientific evidence the judiciary currently lacks.

Fourth, monitor the locus of scientific authority. A critical
precondition for effective public health communication is that the
communicating institution is recognized as a legitimate scientific
authority by its audience. The structural dynamics documented in this
study suggest that this precondition may no longer hold for the KDCA in
the domain of vaccine safety. We recommend systematic monitoring of what
we term the locus of scientific authority --- the source that the public
recognizes as the most credible arbiter of vaccine safety information. A
simple survey item operationalizing this construct might ask: \'When
evaluating the safety of COVID-19 vaccines, whose judgment do you find
most credible?\' with response options including the KDCA, the Board of
Audit and Inspection, the judiciary, independent academic researchers,
international health organizations (WHO/CDC), and online community
sources. Longitudinal tracking of shifts in this distribution would
provide a sensitive early indicator of institutional credibility erosion
and recovery, and would directly inform targeting of communication
interventions.

**4.6 Limitations**

Several limitations of this study warrant acknowledgment. First, the
data are drawn from two Naver channels --- blog posts and news article
comments --- and do not include Naver Café, which constitutes a
quantitatively substantial portion of vaccine-related discourse on the
platform. Café was excluded because the Naver Search API does not return
publication timestamps for Café articles, rendering temporal analysis
impossible. As the central research question concerns event-specific
discourse changes before and after two discrete trigger events, temporal
ordering is analytically indispensable and this exclusion was
unavoidable given current API constraints. It is worth noting that Naver
Café hosts several organized vaccine injury advocacy communities (e.g.,
코백회-affiliated groups), which are disproportionately active in
Compliance (C6) and Conspiracy (C7) discourse. The exclusion of Café may
therefore result in a conservative underestimation of C6 and C7
prevalence relative to the full Naver discourse ecosystem. Furthermore,
the present findings do not represent discourse on platforms such as
YouTube, X (formerly Twitter), or KakaoTalk open channels, and the
extent to which Naver blog and news comment discourse reflects broader
public opinion requires validation against multi-platform data.

Second, the LLM classification pipeline achieved a macro F1 of 0.585 on
the English CAVES benchmark (v1.1.0, claude-haiku-4-5-20251001), with
particularly low performance on C4 (F1 = 0.393) and C7 (F1 = 0.403).
Korean-language classification performance, reported against a 100-item
human-coded gold standard (inter-rater kappa =

**\[XX\]**), reflects comparable limitations. The C1↔C7 boundary
ambiguity identified in the English error analysis is expected to
persist in Korean, as the linguistic markers distinguishing strong
institutional distrust from explicit conspiracy claims are similarly
ambiguous in short social media texts. This limitation is directionally
conservative for our main hypothesis: observed C1+C7 co-occurrence rates
may underestimate true co-occurrence if C7 precision is reduced by
boundary misclassification.

Third, the FM_Direct pre-event stratum had a smaller sampling pool
(n=51) due to the short data collection window prior to the index event,
resulting in a higher within-stratum sampling fraction (33%) compared to
other strata (\<2%). Pre-event FM_Direct estimates should be interpreted
with this constraint in mind.

Fourth, the lead researcher (S.C.) is affiliated with the KDCA, the
institution central to the structural critique developed in this
Discussion. This structural conflict of interest is fully disclosed in
the Ethics Statement. All analytical decisions were pre-registered in
the project plan and subject to independent co-author review to mitigate
potential bias.

**4.7 Future Directions**

The structural analysis presented in this study suggests several
directions for future research. First, longitudinal tracking of the
C1+C7 co-occurrence rate following structural reforms to KDCA\'s
surveillance-compensation architecture --- if such reforms are
implemented --- would provide a natural experiment for testing the
institutional capture hypothesis. A significant reduction in C1+C7
co-occurrence following genuine structural separation would constitute
strong evidence for the proposed mechanism.

Second, comparative studies across jurisdictions with varying degrees of
surveillance--compensation separation would allow direct testing of the
structural prediction. Japan presents a particularly relevant
comparator: the 2013 HPV vaccine suspension, driven by a similar
institutional dynamic of passive surveillance and politically mediated
causality adjudication, produced sustained vaccine hesitancy patterns
that have not been fully reversed despite subsequent WHO endorsement of
HPV vaccine safety.

Third, survey-based measurement of the four Korea-specific constructs
proposed in Sections 4.4 and 4.5 --- perceived procedural injustice in
compensation, conviction of systemic failure, judicial reliance for
safety authority, and locus of scientific authority --- would allow
direct testing of their relationships with 7C dimensions and their
predictive validity for vaccination intentions. In particular, the locus
of scientific authority item may serve as a sensitive moderator of the
C1→C4 cascade: individuals who have transferred authority to the
judiciary are predicted to show higher defensive C4 and C7 scores than
those who retain some residual trust in the KDCA.

Fourth, path analysis of the hypothesized inter-dimensional cascades
would extend the present findings beyond distributional description.
Three specific causal pathways warrant formal testing in future survey
research. The first is the coerced compliance backlash pathway
(C6→C1→C7): mandatory vaccination policies (C6) may have displaced
genuine confidence (C1), creating latent resentment that converts to
conspiratorial belief (C7) upon revelation of institutional safety
failures. The second is the evidence vacuum calculation cascade (C1→C4):
Confidence loss drives individuals toward intensive information-seeking
(C4), but the absence of credible institutional data redirects this
calculation toward judicial and community sources, producing defensive
rather than exploratory C4. The third is the collective responsibility
fragmentation pathway (C5→C4): when the collective sacrifice framing of
mass vaccination (C5) is perceived as unreciprocated --- because the
state contests rather than acknowledges vaccine injuries --- individuals
shift from collective to individual defensive calculative modes (C4),
prioritizing personal legal remedy over community benefit framing.
Structural equation modeling or Bayesian network analysis of
longitudinal panel data would be required to test these directional
hypotheses, as the cross-sectional social media design of the present
study cannot establish causal ordering among dimensions.

**4.8 Conclusion**

This study provides empirical evidence that the 2026 BAI audit
disclosure produced a significant and group-specific shift in the
architecture of vaccine hesitancy discourse on Korean social media,
characterized by a substantial increase in C1+C7 co-occurrence in the
FM_Direct discourse group. We interpret this pattern as reflecting not
primarily individual psychological processes but a structural condition
--- institutional capture of the 7C communication channel --- arising
from the co-location of active surveillance and compensation functions
within a single agency. Under this condition, official safety
disclosures function as evidence-backed conspiracy catalysts rather than
trust-restoring communications, and the judicial system substitutes for
the scientific authority the surveillance infrastructure fails to
produce.

The Korean case offers a critical lesson for global vaccine safety
governance: the quantity of compensation cannot substitute for the
quality of evidence generation. 대한민국 국민은 근거 생산 기관으로서의
질병관리청을 가져야 한다 --- the Korean public deserves a disease
control agency that functions as an evidence-generating institution.
Until that structural condition is realized, the self-reinforcing
distrust loop documented in this study will persist, independent of
communication strategy, compensation generosity, or political will.
