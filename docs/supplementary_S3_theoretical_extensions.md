# Supplementary File S3: Extended Theoretical Discussion

*Dynamics of Vaccine Hesitancy Types Following a Vaccine Safety Crisis:
A 7C Framework Infodemiology Study of Korean Naver Social Media Discourse*

---

## S3.1 Additional Implications for the 7C Framework

The following four theoretical extensions complement the two core extensions
presented in Section 4.4 of the main text. They are reported here in full
to preserve theoretical completeness while maintaining the manuscript's
focus on empirical findings.

**S3.1.1 Institutional antecedents of C1 and C7 co-occurrence**

The surveillance--compensation co-location creates a structural precondition
for C1+C7 co-production that is independent of individual risk perception.
Future applications of the 7C framework in institutional trust contexts
should consider measuring institutional structure variables alongside
individual psychological constructs.

**S3.1.2 The judicial authority transfer variable**

In the absence of credible institutional evidence generation, publics may
transfer scientific authority to judicial bodies. This transfer ---
measurable as increased reliance on court rulings over agency communications
as reference points for vaccine safety --- represents a novel antecedent of
C7 not captured by the original framework. Possible operationalization: 'I
trust the court's judgment on vaccine safety more than KDCA's official
position' as a survey item predicting C7 scores.

**S3.1.3 The temporal transformation of C4: from exploratory to defensive
calculation**

Standard 7C instruments measure C4 as individual cost-benefit reasoning at
the point of vaccination decision. The present findings suggest that in
post-crisis contexts, C4 may undergo a qualitative transformation in which
the information inputs, psychological motivation, and behavioral outputs of
the calculative process are fundamentally altered. Specifically, when
institutional Confidence (C1) collapses and official data loses contextual
credibility, C4 reconfigures from exploratory uncertainty management toward
defensive system verification --- using judicial, audit, and community
sources rather than epidemiological evidence as its primary inputs. This
transformed C4 is characterized by co-occurrence with C1 and C7, and by
orientation toward causal attribution and legal remedy rather than
prospective vaccination decisions. Future 7C measurement instruments applied
in post-crisis institutional trust contexts should consider distinguishing
between these two qualitatively distinct C4 subtypes.

The present findings also raise a question about whether C4 miscalculation
can occur at the institutional level. Korea's Special Act standard ---
accepting temporal proximity without reference to background rates as
sufficient evidence for causal attribution --- represents a potential
institutionalization of the same heuristic bias observed in individual C4
discourse, a dynamic that falls outside the original 7C framework's
individual-level scope and warrants further theoretical development.

**S3.1.4 The retroactive reframing of coerced compliance as a structural
source of grievance discourse**

The 7C framework treats vaccination as a prospective decision point, but
does not account for what may be termed *post-hoc grievance reframing*: the
retrospective reconstruction of a completed vaccination act as involuntary
when adverse outcomes materialize. In the Korean context, this dynamic was
structurally enabled by dual external pressures that compromised the
substantive voluntariness of consent despite formal procedural compliance:
the institutional coercion of the vaccine pass system (방역패스), which
conditioned access to workplaces and public facilities on vaccination status
(Bardosh et al., 2022), and the horizontal conformity pressure of
social media-based peer surveillance (Albrechtslund, 2008). When adverse
events subsequently occurred, the gap between procedural consent and
experienced coercion became the basis for a grievance narrative combining
C1 (loss of confidence in safety assurances), C4 (retrospective
recalculation of an externally imposed decision), and C6 (compliance
experienced as coercion) --- a pattern that standard 7C instruments,
calibrated to prospective vaccination intention, are structurally unable to
capture. A fuller theoretical analysis of this phenomenon, including its
historical and comparative dimensions, is developed elsewhere
(Cheon, in preparation).

## S3.2 Future Directions

The structural analysis presented in this study suggests several directions
for future research.

**S3.2.1 Longitudinal natural experiment**

Longitudinal tracking of the C1+C7 co-occurrence rate following structural
reforms to KDCA's surveillance-compensation architecture --- if such reforms
are implemented --- would provide a natural experiment for testing the
institutional capture hypothesis. A significant reduction in C1+C7
co-occurrence following genuine structural separation would constitute
strong evidence for the proposed mechanism.

**S3.2.2 Cross-jurisdictional comparative studies**

Comparative studies across jurisdictions with varying degrees of
surveillance--compensation separation would allow direct testing of the
structural prediction. Japan presents a particularly relevant comparator:
the 2013 HPV vaccine suspension, driven by a similar institutional dynamic
of passive surveillance and politically mediated causality adjudication,
produced sustained vaccine hesitancy patterns that have not been fully
reversed despite subsequent WHO endorsement of HPV vaccine safety.

**S3.2.3 Survey-based construct validation**

Survey-based measurement of the Korea-specific constructs proposed in
Sections 4.4 and 4.5 of the main text --- perceived procedural injustice in
compensation, conviction of systemic failure, judicial reliance for safety
authority, and locus of scientific authority --- would allow direct testing
of their relationships with 7C dimensions and their predictive validity for
vaccination intentions. In particular, the locus of scientific authority
item may serve as a sensitive moderator of the C1→C4 cascade: individuals
who have transferred authority to the judiciary are predicted to show higher
defensive C4 and C7 scores than those who retain some residual trust in the
KDCA.

**S3.2.4 Path analysis of inter-dimensional cascades**

Path analysis of the hypothesized inter-dimensional cascades would extend
the present findings beyond distributional description. Three specific
causal pathways warrant formal testing in future survey research. The first
is the coerced compliance backlash pathway (C6→C1→C7): mandatory
vaccination policies (C6) may have displaced genuine confidence (C1),
creating latent resentment that converts to conspiratorial belief (C7) upon
revelation of institutional safety failures. The second is the evidence
vacuum calculation cascade (C1→C4): Confidence loss drives individuals
toward intensive information-seeking (C4), but the absence of credible
institutional data redirects this calculation toward judicial and community
sources, producing defensive rather than exploratory C4. The third is the
collective responsibility fragmentation pathway (C5→C4): when the
collective sacrifice framing of mass vaccination (C5) is perceived as
unreciprocated --- because the state contests rather than acknowledges
vaccine injuries --- individuals shift from collective to individual
defensive calculative modes (C4), prioritizing personal legal remedy over
community benefit framing. Structural equation modeling or Bayesian network
analysis of longitudinal panel data would be required to test these
directional hypotheses, as the cross-sectional social media design of the
present study cannot establish causal ordering among dimensions.
