# LinkedIn Post & Card News: Infovail-IQ Announcement

## 📱 LinkedIn Post (English Only)

**[Title: Beyond Sentiment: Decoding the Architecture of Distrust with Infovail-IQ]**

[cite_start]I am incredibly inspired by the recent groundbreaking research on **PH-LLM** (Public Health Large Language Models) led by Yuan Luo and the team at Northwestern University[cite: 5]. Their work, recently accepted in *npj Digital Medicine*, demonstrates that domain-specific models can significantly outperform general-purpose LLMs in public health tasks.

This research was the direct "aha moment" for my new project: **Infovail-IQ**. 

While traditional sentiment analysis captures *what* people feel, we need to understand the underlying **structure of distrust** to protect public health. **Infovail-IQ** is a 4-layer infoveillance framework designed to bridge the gap between raw social data and strategic policy action by automating the **WHO CAVES framework**.

**The 4 Layers of Infovail-IQ Intelligence:**

1.  **Layer 1: Aggregation (The Baseline)** – Real-time tracking of sentiments, CAVES categories, and misinformation.
2.  **Layer 2: Co-occurrence (The Structure)** – Mapping how specific concerns (e.g., *Government Distrust + Safety Concerns*) cluster together to identify the root causes of hesitancy.
3.  **Layer 3: Temporal Dynamics (The Timing)** – Evaluating the impact of official interventions by tracking opinion shifts before and after key events.
4.  **Layer 4: Policy Intelligence (The Action)** – Generating actionable briefings with behavioral directives for decision-makers.

A core priority of this project is **global accessibility**. By leveraging the compact **PH-LLM-3B** model for local inference, **Infovail-IQ** aims to provide a cost-effective solution for Low- and Middle-Income Countries (LMICs).

I am currently conducting a retrospective evaluation against real-world crisis data and plan to submit our methodological findings to journals like **JMIR** or **IJMI**. I welcome any insights from the global health and informatics community!
